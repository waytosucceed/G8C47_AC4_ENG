Claim:
Stricter regulations on smartphone use in class should be implemented because it minimises distractions and enhances focus. Studies have shown that students who are restricted from using smartphones during lessons demonstrate better engagement and higher academic achievement. Limiting phone use also fosters stronger interpersonal interactions, encouraging students to collaborate and communicate effectively with peers.

Counterclaim:
Critics argue that smartphones are valuable learning tools, providing instant access to educational apps, research materials, and collaborative platforms. Banning them entirely might hinder students from utilising these resources to enhance their understanding of complex subjects.

Conclusion:
While smartphones can be beneficial in certain contexts, the negative impact of their misuse on concentration and productivity supports the need for stricter regulations. By implementing clear policies, schools can strike a balance between leveraging technology for learning and maintaining an environment conducive to academic success.
